# Workflow & Competitor Analysis

This document categorizes the core workflows mapped in the persona journeys and highlights how the Event Intelligence Platform will outmaneuver existing incumbents (Cvent, Brella, Swapcard, Grip, Airmeet, Hubilo).

## 1. High-Frequency Workflows
*These are actions performed constantly. They must be frictionless.*

### Attendee Discovery (Pre-Event)
*   **Current Workflow:** Scrolling through an alphabetical list of attendees to find someone relevant.
*   **Competitor Solution:** Grip & Swapcard offer basic tag-based matchmaking during the event window.
*   **Our Opportunity:** **AI-Native Discovery.** Instead of a list, the attendee sees an AI-curated "For You" feed powered by their global Event Memory (people they met at past events, or people with similar trajectories).

### In-App Messaging (During Event)
*   **Current Workflow:** Sending a text message inside the event app.
*   **Competitor Solution:** Standard chat features that delete or lock down a week after the event (Brella/Cvent).
*   **Our Opportunity:** **Persistent Threads.** Chats do not disappear. The thread transitions into a year-round networking channel, capturing the post-event relationship.

## 2. High-Value Workflows
*These workflows generate the most business value for the participants.*

### Lead Capture & Qualification (Exhibitors)
*   **Current Workflow:** Scanning a QR code on a badge and typing notes into a phone.
*   **Competitor Solution:** Cvent LeadCapture (expensive rented hardware or basic app scanners).
*   **Our Opportunity:** **AI-Native Copilot Lead Capture.** The exhibitor speaks into their phone ("Just met with John, he wants a demo in Q3, budget is $50k"), and the AI instantly parses it into structured CRM fields, scores the lead, and queues the follow-up email.

### Meeting Scheduling (Attendees/Sponsors)
*   **Current Workflow:** Ping-ponging messages to find a time to meet at Table 5.
*   **Competitor Solution:** Brella excels at strict 15-minute matchmaking slots.
*   **Our Opportunity:** **Frictionless Availability.** Auto-syncing with external calendars (Google/Outlook) and auto-assigning locations based on dynamic capacity, removing the back-and-forth entirely.

## 3. Revenue-Generating Workflows
*These workflows directly drive platform monetization.*

### Sponsorship Attribution (Post-Event)
*   **Current Workflow:** An organizer emails a PDF to a sponsor showing "estimated foot traffic."
*   **Competitor Solution:** Airmeet provides virtual booth metrics, but struggles with physical/hybrid attribution.
*   **Our Opportunity:** **Digital-Grade ROI.** We provide a real-time analytics dashboard showing exactly how many people matching the sponsor's target persona clicked their ad or visited their booth. We transition from selling "Gold/Silver packages" to "Pay-Per-Lead" or SaaS subscriptions for continuous sponsor intelligence.

### Ticketing & Registration (Before Event)
*   **Current Workflow:** Buying a ticket via a static form.
*   **Competitor Solution:** Cvent owns enterprise ticketing; Eventbrite owns SMB.
*   **Our Opportunity:** **Intelligence Upsells.** We process the core ticket transaction (base revenue), but up-sell AI features ("Pay $50 extra for Premium Matchmaking and Lead Export") directly in the checkout flow.

## 4. The Differentiating Workflow (Our Moat)
*This is the workflow where incumbents cannot compete due to their architectural legacy.*

### The Event Memory Transition (After Event)
*   **Current Workflow:** The event ends. The app is deleted. Data is exported to CSVs.
*   **Competitor Solution (Cvent/Hubilo):** They optimize for the Organizer's operations. The event is a silo. Once it ends, the data is archived.
*   **Competitor Solution (Brella/Swapcard):** They optimize for networking *during* the event, but the graph resets for the next event.
*   **Our Opportunity:** **The Continuous Graph.** When the event ends, the platform shifts into "Community Mode." The attendee's profile, connections, and interactions persist globally. When they attend a different event hosted by a different organizer next year, their Event Memory travels with them. The platform becomes the unified system of record for their professional network.
